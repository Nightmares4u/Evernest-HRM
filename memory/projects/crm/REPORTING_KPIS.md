# Reporting and KPIs

## Reporting Principle

The CRM should help management answer:

- Where did leads come from?
- Who owns them?
- Are they being followed up?
- Which campaigns produce quality leads?
- Which branches and agents convert?
- Which countries/products generate revenue?
- Where are cases stuck?
- What requires management action today?

- Invoices sent

- Active cases

- Revenue
- Estimated gross profit

- Average response time
- Overdue follow-ups
- Stale leads

## Daily Management Snapshot

The future daily summary should show:

- New leads yesterday/today
- Leads assigned
- Unassigned leads
- Hot leads
- Overdue follow-ups
- Invoices sent
- Payments received
- Documents pending
- Active cases stuck
- Campaign spend and results if available
- Branch/agent exceptions

## Reporting Phases

MVP reports:

- Lead volume by branch, agent, campaign, product
- Follow-up due and overdue
- Qualified and converted counts
- Basic invoice and payment totals
- Active cases and document pending counts

Post-MVP reports:

- Campaign ROI
- Approval/refusal ratios
- Product profitability
- Commission-ready exports
- Attendance versus productivity
- AI-assisted management summaries
